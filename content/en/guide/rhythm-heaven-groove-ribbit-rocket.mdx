<Section icon={Target} title="Gameplay Mechanics and Frog Patterns">

The gameplay loop of **rhythm heaven groove ribbit rocket** is elegant but demands strict focus. Frogs hop onto the screen from the left, bouncing across a series of four lilypads. The player controls the launcher under the fourth lilypad. 

Your primary objective is to push the lilypad upward at the exact moment the frog lands on it, launching them back off-screen into the sky. However, the game will frequently throw curveballs to test your internal rhythm.

### Frog Behaviors and Variations

*   **Standard Frogs**: These frogs hop sequentially across all four lilypads on the main beat. They establish the baseline tempo.
*   **Skipping Frogs**: Sometimes, incoming frogs will skip some of the initial lilypads, landing directly on the third or fourth pad earlier than expected. This forces you to react quickly to a sudden rhythm shift.
*   **Red Frogs**: These are rare, highly tricky frogs that hop on offbeats. They require you to strike the button in between the standard beats.

| Frog Type | Movement Pattern | Timing Strategy | Difficulty |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Standard (Green)** | Hops sequentially on 1-2-3-4 | Press on the steady 4th beat | Easy |
| **Skipping (Green)** | Skips pads, landing early | Anticipate the shortened gap | Medium |
| **Red Frog** | Jumps on offbeats | Press off-beat precisely | Hard |

<Callout type="warning" title="Watch the Offbeats">
Do not let the visual movement of the red frogs distract you. Focus entirely on the syncopated audio cue to hit the offbeat launch perfectly.
</Callout>
