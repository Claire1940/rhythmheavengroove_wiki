<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Understanding the Night Mode Mechanics">

The release of the game on July 2, 2026, introduced players to a fresh set of rhythmic challenges. Among the most demanding additions is the high-difficulty alternate campaign. This mode alters the visual presentation of the standard stages, shifting them to neon-drenched, nighttime settings. However, the changes are not merely cosmetic. 

In this mode, the tempo of each mini-game is increased, and the visual cues are frequently obscured by shadows, flashing neon lights, or weather effects like rain and fog. This design choice forces players to rely almost entirely on their auditory memory and rhythmic synchronization. The timing window for securing a Perfect or Superb rating is significantly reduced, requiring precise button presses.

| Feature | Day Mode | Night Mode |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Visual Cues** | Clear, bright, and easy to follow | Obscured by shadows, neon flashes, and weather |
| **Tempo Multiplier** | 1.0x standard speed | 1.15x to 1.35x speed depending on the stage |
| **Timing Window** | Generous (approx. 50ms for Superb) | Tight (approx. 25ms for Superb) |

| **Failure Tolerance** | Allows up to 3 major misses | Often triggers an instant fail or strict rating drop |
