<Section icon={Shield} title="Advanced Tips for High Scores">

Getting a "Perfect" rating in **rhythm heaven groove multiplayer** requires more than just looking at the screen. The developers at Nintendo and TNX designed these games to trick your visual senses.

### Timing Precision Guide

| Rating | Timing Window | Visual Feedback | Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Perfect** | Under 15ms | Bright golden spark | Close your eyes and tap purely to the audio track rhythm. |
| **Good** | 16ms - 50ms | Silver flash | Keep a steady physical bounce or nod to maintain the tempo. |
| **OK** | 51ms - 100ms | Standard hit cue | Adjust your timing if you are rushing ahead of the beat. |
| **Miss** | Over 100ms | Red cross / Fail sound | Listen to your teammates' button clicks to find the groove again. |

### Pro Strategies for Success
- **Count Aloud**: Especially in games like *Cake Wait*, counting the beats out loud (1, 2, 3, Go!) helps synchronize the entire room.
- **Watch the Audio Cues**: Visual distractions are intentionally placed on screen. Rely on the distinct sound effects unique to each mini-game.
- **Learn the Call-and-Response**: Many games use a teacher-student format. Let Player 1 set the tempo, and have subsequent players mirror the exact rhythm.
