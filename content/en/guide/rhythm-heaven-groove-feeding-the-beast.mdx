<Section icon={Target} title="Gameplay Mechanics & Control Schemes">

Understanding the controls is the first step to conquering **rhythm heaven groove feeding the beast**. True to the series' roots, the control scheme is beautifully simple, relying primarily on a single button press to match the rhythm. However, the timing windows are strict, demanding precision to achieve an "Amazing!" rating or secure a Perfect campaign.

<Callout type="tip" title="Practice Feature">
If your timing feels off, do not jump straight into the main game. On the tutorial screen, hold down the **Ⓨ button** to watch a perfect AI demonstration. This helps your ears adjust to the syncopation before you play.
</Callout>

### Control Inputs

| Input Button | Action in Game | Timing Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Ⓐ Button** | Chomp / Bite | Press exactly on the beat to eat the incoming flower buds. |
| **Ⓨ Button** | Watch Example | Hold during the tutorial screen to view a perfect demonstration. |
| **✚ Pad / Ⓑ Button** | Menu Navigation | Used for navigating screens; not active during active gameplay. |

<Section icon={Swords} title="How to Get an Amazing Medal & Perfect Rating">

Earning an "Amazing!" medal requires hitting almost every single beat perfectly, while a Perfect run allows for zero mistakes. In **rhythm heaven groove feeding the beast**, the visual cues can actually trick your brain because the camera angles shift as the sun sets. 

To succeed, you must rely entirely on your auditory senses and ignore the distracting animations.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Listen to the Vocals">
    The Japanese vocal track provides direct cues. Phrases like \"Give it to me!\" and rhythmic chanting align perfectly with when you should press Ⓐ.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Master the Off-Beats">
    This stage relies heavily on syncopation. Many flower buds must be eaten on the half-beat or backbeat rather than the main downbeat.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Calibrate System Latency">
    Wireless controllers or modern TVs can introduce audio lag. Go to the settings menu to calibrate your timing if you keep getting \"Miss\" ratings.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning" title="Visual Traps">
As the song progresses, the background changes from bright daylight to a dark sunset. Do not let the darkening screen or the bouncing flower animations break your focus. Keep your eyes relaxed and focus on the steady tempo.
</Callout>

### Scoring & Performance Tiers

Your performance is graded at the end of the song based on your accuracy.

| Rating | Reward Unlocked | Accuracy Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Try Again** | None | Missed too many beats; background song fails early. |
| **OK** | Next Game Unlocked | Hit most major beats, but missed several off-beats. |
| **Amazing!** | Medal Awarded | High accuracy; missed fewer than 3-4 beats overall. |
| **Perfect** | Perfect Gift / Ring | Completed the random Perfect Campaign challenge with zero misses. |

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Feeding the Beast 2 & Hard Mode Variations">

Once you master the base game in Stage 1, the challenge escalates significantly later in the campaign. In Stage 15, you will encounter **Feeding the Beast 2**, which pushes your rhythmic reflexes to their absolute limits.

### Key Differences: Version 1 vs. Version 2

| Feature | Feeding the Beast (Stage 1) | Feeding the Beast 2 (Stage 15) |
|---|---|---|
| **Tempo** | Moderate, steady J-pop beat | Accelerated, high-bpm electronic track |
| **Pattern Complexity** | Single and double consecutive chomps | Rapid-fire triple chomps and off-beat pauses |
| **Visual Distractions** | Simple day-to-night background shift | Flashing neon lights and camera zoom-ins |
| **Unlocks** | Unlocks Remix 1 and the Cafe | Required to unlock the final Remix stages |

<Callout type="success" title="Pro Tip for Version 2">
In Feeding the Beast 2, try lightly tapping your foot on every single quarter-beat. Even when there are no flower buds coming, keeping a physical pulse will prevent you from rushing during the sudden rapid-fire sequences.
</Callout>
