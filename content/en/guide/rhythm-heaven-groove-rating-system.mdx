<Section icon={Target} title="Understanding Grade Ranks and Performance Tiers">

Every rhythm game in the collection concludes with a summary screen. The **rhythm heaven groove rating system** categorizes your performance into four distinct tiers. These tiers are not just for show; they directly impact your progression through the game’s map and the unlocking of bonus content.

| Grade Rank | Performance Requirement | Primary Reward | Progression Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Try Again** | High number of misses/barely hits | No Reward | Stage remains uncleared |
| **OK** | Decent timing with several mistakes | Flow Ball (Small) | Unlocks the next stage |
| **Superb** | Near-perfect timing, few mistakes | **Medal** | Unlocks Beatspell chapters |
| **Perfect** | Zero missed inputs (Campaign only) | Gift / Certificate | Full completion status |

The "OK" rank is the minimum requirement to move forward, but the "Superb" rank is what most players should aim for. Earning a Medal signifies that you have mastered the specific rhythm of that stage, such as the "pa pi pu pe po" pattern in *Hoop Trundling*.

<Callout type="success" title="Success Tip">
Achieving a Superb rank often requires you to ignore visual distractions. In games like *Fruit Flex*, the background animations are designed to throw off your timing. Trust your ears!
</Callout>

<Callout type="info" title="Medal Tracking">
You can track your total Medals in the main menu. These are the primary currency for the **Beatspell mode**, where specific chapter milestones require a set number of Medals to enter.
</Callout>

Once you have secured a Superb rank on a stage, the **rhythm heaven groove rating system** occasionally triggers a "Perfect Campaign." This is a high-stakes challenge where a single mistake results in an immediate failure. The game gives you three attempts per campaign before the opportunity disappears, though it will eventually return.

| Reward Type | Acquisition Method | Usage |
