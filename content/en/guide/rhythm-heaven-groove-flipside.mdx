### How Beatspell Combat Works

Unlike the standard arcade modes, Beatspell features a structured battle system:

- **Rhythm Inputs**: You are given a spell scroll displaying a sequence of beats. Tapping the sequence correctly casts spells like Fireball or Lightning.
- **Enemy Turns**: Monsters attack with their own rhythmic patterns. You must press the shield button exactly on their attack beats to block damage.
- **Boss Fights**: Bosses change tempos mid-battle or use silence mechanics, forcing you to rely entirely on your internal metronome.

<Callout type="info" title="How to Unlock Beatspell">
Beatspell Mode is not available immediately. You must progress through the first few sets of standard solo minigames and earn at least 5 silver medals to unlock the opening story chapter.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Controls, Audio Calibration, and Accessibility">

Rhythm games require absolute precision. Even a millisecond of input lag can turn a "Superb" rating into a "Miss." **Rhythm Heaven Groove** features robust system settings to help you optimize your setup.

### Essential Calibration Steps

If you are playing on a modern television, display processing can introduce audio-visual lag. Follow these steps to calibrate your game:

1. Navigate to the **Settings** menu from the main screen.
2. Select **Audio/Video Calibration**.
3. Tap the button along with the metronome clicks on the screen.
4. The system will calculate your display lag and automatically adjust the input window.
5. Save settings. If you switch from handheld to TV mode, repeat this process.

### Game Accessibility Options

Nintendo has introduced several helpful accessibility features to ensure everyone can enjoy the music:

- **Li'l Miss Reeds Voice Assistant**: An optional voice-over assistant that reads out-of-game menus and on-screen text instructions.
- **Visual Rhythm Indicators**: Toggle on-screen flash prompts to assist players who prefer visual feedback over audio cues.
- **One-Button Control Mode**: Simplify complex minigames by mapping all actions to a single button press, focusing purely on tempo rather than button layouts.

<Callout type="warning" title="Wireless Controller Lag">
When playing in local multiplayer, wireless Joy-Con controllers can occasionally experience minor interference. For the best competitive results, play in TV mode with controllers close to the console dock.
</Callout>
