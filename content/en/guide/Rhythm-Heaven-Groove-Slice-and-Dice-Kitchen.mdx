<Section icon={Swords} title="Core Gameplay Mechanics and Cues">

To master **Rhythm Heaven Groove Slice and Dice Kitchen**, you must familiarize yourself with how different ingredients behave. Each vegetable thrown has a specific rhythm, visual appearance, and audio signature.

### Ingredient Rhythm Rules

| Vegetable | Throw Pattern | Beat Alignment | Audio Cue |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tomato** | Single or sequence of three | Standard on-beat | Standard humming |
| **Yellow Pepper** | Single throw | Standard on-beat | Accompanied by a timer beat |
| **Broccoli** | Fast set of three | Half-beat (off-beat) | Triggered immediately after the Yellow Pepper |

The core challenge of the minigame lies in the transition between standard on-beat tomatoes and the fast, off-beat triplets of broccoli. The yellow pepper serves as your visual and auditory warning system. When you see a yellow pepper and hear the ticking timer beat, prepare your thumb for a rapid succession of three taps to catch the oncoming broccoli.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Guide to a Perfect Rating">

Earning a "Perfect!" or "Ace!" rating requires absolute precision. Follow these steps to train your muscle memory and clear the stage flawlessly.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Base Beat">
    At the start of the minigame, do not focus on the character's hands. Instead, bob your head or tap your foot to the steady background rhythm. Listen closely to the woman's introductory humming to get a feel for the tempo.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master the Single and Triple Tomatoes">
    Single tomatoes require a single press of the **A Button** exactly on the beat. When a sequence of three tomatoes is thrown, maintain a steady 1-2-3 tapping rhythm without speeding up.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="React to the Yellow Pepper Timer">
    When the yellow pepper flies in, listen for the distinct **timer beat**. This is your cue that the fast broccoli triplet is next. Keep your hand relaxed and prepare for off-beat inputs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Broccoli Catch">
    As soon as the timer beat finishes, tap the **A Button** three times rapidly on the half-beats. The timing is much faster than the tomato sequence, requiring quick, light taps.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Ignore Visual Color Distractions">
    When the kitchen lights change color and obscure the screen, do not panic. Maintain your established tapping patterns. Relying purely on the audio cues will prevent you from mistiming your catches.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Timing Windows and Feedback">

The game provides immediate visual feedback based on your input timing. Knowing these indicators will help you adjust your rhythm mid-game.

### Performance Feedback System

| Rating | Catch Hand | Visual Result | Audio Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Perfect! / Ace!** | Left Hand | Vegetable is cleanly caught | Crisp catching sound |
| **Early! / Late!** | None | Vegetable bounces off the hand | Thud sound / Cat paw appears |

If your timing is slightly off, the vegetable will bounce off the chef's hand and fall to the kitchen floor. A small cat will quickly stick its paw out from the bottom of the screen to catch the dropped ingredient. While adorable, seeing the cat means your Perfect run has ended, and you will need to restart the stage to claim the medal.

<Callout type="info" title="Auditory Cue Priority">
If you find yourself struggling with the visual transitions, try looking slightly away from the center of the screen. Keeping the chef in your peripheral vision forces your brain to prioritize the audio track, which is the most reliable way to conquer any *Rhythm Heaven* minigame.
</Callout>
