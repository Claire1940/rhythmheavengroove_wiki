<Section icon={Target} title="Controls and Timing Tips for Beginners">

Mastering the controls in Rhythm Heaven Groove requires a mental shift. The game often uses visual distractions—like zooming cameras or obscuring objects—to try and throw you off the beat. The most successful players are those who can close their eyes and still play the game perfectly based solely on the audio.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Calibration">
    Start by visiting the settings menu. Use the built-in calibration tool to ensure the sound and button inputs are perfectly synced with your display's refresh rate.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master the Cues">
    Play each tutorial carefully. Listen for the specific sound effects (cues) that precede an action. For example, in Hoop Trundling, the vocal cue "pa-pi-pu-pe-po" tells you exactly when to jump on the final beat.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Ignore Visual Traps">
    When the screen begins to shake or the characters move off-screen, maintain your rhythm. The music never changes its tempo unless the game explicitly warns you with an audio signal.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Practice in the Demo">
    Use the five available solo games in the Starter Demo to build muscle memory. These games are curated to represent the fundamental timing windows you will encounter in the full 80-game set.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Warning">
Wireless headsets or Bluetooth speakers can introduce significant audio lag. For the best experience, use the Switch's built-in speakers or a wired pair of headphones.
</Callout>
