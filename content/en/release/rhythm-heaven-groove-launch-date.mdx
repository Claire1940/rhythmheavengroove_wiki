<Section icon={Trophy} title="Local Multiplayer Setup & Modes">

The local multiplayer mode supports up to four players on a single Nintendo Switch system. It features over 30 unique rhythm games designed specifically for cooperative play and friendly competition. Note that the game does not support online multiplayer or local wireless play between multiple consoles; all multiplayer action takes place on a single screen.

To play with friends, each player must have a compatible controller. A single pair of Joy-Con controllers can be split between two players for quick sessions, but a four-player game requires additional controllers, such as Pro Controllers or extra Joy-Cons.

| Multiplayer Mode | Player Count | Style | Controller Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cooperative Challenges** | 2-4 Players | Shared score, alternating beats | One controller per player |
| **Competitive Battles** | 2-4 Players | High-score tracking, speed tests | One controller per player |
| **Cake Wait** | 1-4 Players | Timing-based survival | Joy-Con or Pro Controller |
| **Tennis Quest** | 1-2 Players | Cooperative monster combat | Joy-Con or Pro Controller |

Cooperative games like Rhythm Tweezers require players to alternate plucking hairs from a giant onion, demanding perfect synchronization. Competitive modes like Cake Wait challenge players to grab a slice of cake exactly as the clock strikes three, rewarding the player with the most precise timing.

<Callout type="info" title="Switch Lite Compatibility">
Nintendo Switch Lite users can enjoy local multiplayer modes by syncing compatible external controllers (such as Joy-Cons or Pro Controllers) to the system.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Beatspell Rhythm RPG Mode">

Beatspell is a unique, unlockable single-player mode that blends traditional rhythm timing with classic RPG progression. Instead of simply clearing stages for high scores, players use rhythmic button inputs to cast spells, heal wounds, and defeat monsters in turn-based battles. Earning medals in standard single-player minigames is required to unlock new chapters and abilities in this mode.
