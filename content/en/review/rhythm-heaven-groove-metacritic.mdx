<Section icon={Shield} title="The Input Lag Problem: Handheld vs. Docked Performance">

The single most consistent warning across almost all **Rhythm Heaven Groove Metacritic** reviews is the presence of input lag when playing on a docked television. Because the game demands frame-perfect button presses, even a millisecond of display latency can turn a perfect run into a frustrating miss.

To ensure the best possible experience, reviewers have outlined clear guidelines on how to play:

| Play Mode | Performance Rating | Best Use Case | Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Handheld Mode** | **★★★★★ (Excellent)** | Solo progression, chasing perfect ranks | Highly recommended; zero display latency. |
| **Tabletop Mode** | **★★★★☆ (Very Good)** | Local multiplayer on the go | Great alternative; avoids TV processing lag. |
| **Docked TV Mode** | **★★★☆☆ (Average)** | Group party nights, casual play | Requires extensive display and audio calibration. |

If you must play on a docked TV, you should immediately run the built-in **TV Calibration** tool in the settings menu [00首页信息-2]. This utility adjusts the timing window to compensate for your display's internal latency [00首页信息-2]. Additionally, setting your television to "Game Mode" and using wired controllers can significantly reduce input lag.

<Callout type="warning" title="Audio Latency Warning">
Avoid using Bluetooth headphones or wireless soundbars when playing docked. The cumulative audio delay from wireless protocols will severely disrupt your rhythm and timing.
</Callout>
